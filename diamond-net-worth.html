    Diamond started her career in music at a very young age, becoming part of Crime Mob at the age of fifteen. They released the single “Knuck if You Buck” which became very popular, eventually certified Platinum, and earning the group national recognition, after which the group would release their first, self-titled album. This continued with a few more song releases, and then they worked on their second album – “Hated on Mostly” – releasing it during 2007.

    In late 2007, Diamond decided to pursue a solo career and left Crime Mob. One of her first appearances as a solo artist was on a remix of Ludacris’ “My Chick Bad”, which featured her along with Eve and Trina. She then released her first single entitled “Lotta Money” in 2010. The following year, she released another music video entitled “Hit That Hoe” which featured Waka Flocka. In 2011 she released “Buy It All”, and it earned her a nomination for Best Female Hip Hop Artist during the 2011 BET Awards. The following year, she released another single “Loose Screws” and got herself another nomination at the BET Awards. Her album, “The Young Life”, was released in August 2012, which had songs including “Love Like Mine” and “American Woman”. One of her recent projects is the reality television show “Sisterhood of Hip-Hop” which is produced by Oxygen; the show features artists such as Bia, Nyemiah Supreme, and Brianna Perry.

    Aside from her music career, Diamond is very popular on social media, boasting over 500,000 followers on Instagram. She also has over 200,000 followers on Twitter. Her accounts often display upcoming projects and shows.

    For her personal life, it is known that she was previously in a relationship with Soulja Boy, and is currently in a relationship with Payro. Diamond names numerous influences for her music, including Missy Elliot, Timbaland, Snoop Dogg, and Tupac Shakur. She has her own website and is often seen performing with other artists. Recently, Diamond gained a lot of controversy for comments made about Scrappy’s mother on the show “Sisterhood of Hip-Hop” – she’s been threatened with being sued for slander. Diamond also recently underwent a manager change.
