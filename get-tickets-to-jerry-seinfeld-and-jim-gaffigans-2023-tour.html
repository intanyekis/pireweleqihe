    ‘Tis the year of the co-headliner.

    Over the past few months, many of the biggest names in comedy — like Tina Fey with Amy Poehler, Steve Martin with Martin Short and John Mulaney with Pete Davidson — have teamed up to put on massive two-performer shows.

    Next up, observational comics Jerry Seinfeld and Jim Gaffigan will share the stage on a six-show arena tour from Nov. 2-11 where they’ll hit San Francisco, Inglewood, Chicago and St. Louis.

    “I’m so excited to help out this unknown up and upcoming comedian who many may have never heard of,” the 57-year-old Gaffigan joked. “I know it will be an amazing bunch of shows.”

    As of now, seats are available for all gigs on the brief run.

    Some are quite affordable, too.

    At the time of publication, our team found tickets going for as low as $53 before fees on Vivid Seats.

    For NY and NJ based fans that can’t make the last-minute tour, you don’t need to worry.

    Seinfeld, 69, is playing Albany’s Palace Theatre on Oct. 27, Atlantic City’s Borgata Casino Event Center on Dec. 1 and Syracuse’s Landmark Theatre on Dec. 8.

    Gaffigan just announced more dates for his ‘Barely Alive Tour’ that will stop into Buffalo’s Sheas Performing Arts Center on Feb. 9-10 and conclude at New York City’s Beacon Theatre on April 26-27.
