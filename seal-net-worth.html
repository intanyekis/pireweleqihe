    Minor performances in nearby clubs and pubs were the beginning of Seal’s musical career. He joined the British funk group “Push” in 1987, and the group took him on international tours. Before leaving for England, he took a solo trip of India.

    He eventually agreed to a contract with “ZTT Records” and issued his debut album under that name. The Trevor Horn-produced album was made available in 1991. Both critics and his fans praised each of his singles from the album “Seal.”

    Seal achieved his first hat-trick of victories at the 1992 Brit Awards, which were hosted at London’s Hammersmith Odeon. He won prizes for “Best British Album,” “Best British Video,” and “Best British Male” (all for “Seal”).

    He released the collection of all of his hits, “Seal: Best 1991-2004,” in 2004. Additionally, it featured renditions of the songs “Walk on By” by Bacharach and David and “Lips Like Sugar” by Echo & the Bunnymen.
