    NBA fans have been deeply saddened by the passing of professional basketball player Eric Montross, and have expressed their heartfelt condolences to his wife and family. Eric Montross, formerly a star for the Toronto Raptors, was cherished by his family, friends, fans, and well-wishers throughout his life. He had a strong and enduring marriage with Laura Montross.

    Eric Montross was born into an athletic family on September 23, 1971, in Indianapolis, Indiana, United States. He began his athletic journey at Lawrence North High School, showcasing exceptional abilities. Later, he attended the University of North Carolina in Chapel Hill, where he joined the Tar Heels and achieved recognition as an All-American in his junior and senior years. He played a pivotal role in UNC’s 1993 NCAA Championship victory against Michigan. He was subsequently drafted by the Boston Celtics as the ninth overall pick in the 1994 NBA draft. Eric Montross played for various teams including the Boston Celtics, New Jersey Nets, Dallas Mavericks, Philadelphia 76ers, Toronto Raptors, and Detroit Pistons. Sadly, Eric Montross passed away on December 17, 2023, at the age of 52, leaving behind his wife and children.

    Discovering Laura Montross, Eric Montross’ Wife: Notable Information

    Eric, the celebrated player and color commentator, shared a lasting marriage with Laura Montross. Their union began in 1994, and they remained dedicated to each other for over three decades. Laura, originally Laura Leonard, accompanied Eric to various cities due to his professional commitments, including Boston, Dallas, New Jersey, Philadelphia, Detroit, and Toronto. A native of Lexington, North Carolina, Laura Montross retains a subtle Southern accent.

    While her exact date of birth remains undisclosed, she completed her degree at UNC in 1993. Despite her and her husband’s considerable influence, she maintains a genuinely humble and down-to-earth demeanor. Laura co-founded the annual tennis fundraising event, Raise a Racquet, for UNC Children’s Hospital at The FARM. She also played a role in establishing the Arts in Action program at Frank Porter Graham Elementary School. Currently, Laura serves on the boards of directors for Be Loud! and the UNC Children’s Hospital Board of Visitors.

    Understanding Eric’s Passing

    The Montross family announced that Eric Montross passed away at his Chapel Hill residence, surrounded by loved ones, on December 17. UNC basketball confirmed his passing the following morning. In the latter part of March 2023, the Montross family revealed Eric’s cancer diagnosis. He received treatment at the UNC Lineberger Cancer Center.

    Regrettably, after battling cancer, Eric could not endure and passed away. His family expressed gratitude to the medical community, particularly those at UNC Lineberger Comprehensive Cancer Center, for their unwavering support during his struggle. They also appreciated the tremendous support and overwhelming love demonstrated by numerous individuals throughout his battle. The Montross family requested privacy during this challenging time. Carolina Athletics described him as a man of faith, an exceptional parent, husband, and son, and as one of the most distinguished University and Chapel Hill ambassadors.
