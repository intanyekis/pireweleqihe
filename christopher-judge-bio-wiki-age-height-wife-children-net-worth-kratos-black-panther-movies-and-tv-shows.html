    Christopher Judge Biography

    Christopher Judge is an American actor who was born and brought up in Los Angeles, California. He is famous for playing Teal’c in the Canadian-American military science fiction television series Stargate SG-1, Kratos in the 2018 video game God of War, and King T’Challa/Black Panther in August 2021 Marvel’s Avengers video game.

    Judge has also been featured in other films and tv series Andromeda, Stargate Atlantis, The Mentalist, the television movie Personal Effects, and the films Snow Dogs and A Dog’s Breakfast, and in the 2012 film The Dark Knight Rises.

    Christopher Judge Actor

    Christopher desired to be an actor since his childhood. His first tv appearance in the 90s include Bird on a Wire, Cadence, Neon Rider, and MacGyver.

    In the following years, Judge played minor roles in various tv shows and movies such as 21 Jump Street, The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air, and House Party 2, and as a regular on Sirens from 1994 to 1995.

    His major role came in 1997 with Stargate SG-1. His subsequent work includes guest spots on Andromeda, Stargate Atlantis, The Mentalist, the television movie Personal Effects, and the films Snow Dogs and A Dog’s Breakfast, the latter written and directed by fellow Stargate actor David Hewlett.

    Judge appeared in “Anonymous”, the October 26, 2010 episode of NCIS: Los Angeles, and as one of Bane’s henchmen in the 2012 film The Dark Knight Rises.

    Christopher Judge Kratos

    in 2018, Judge lent his voice to Kratos, a character in God of War, an action-adventure game developed by Santa Monica Studio. He received the 2019 D.I.C.E. Awards, for Outstanding Achievement in Character for his work as Kratos in God of War.
